Commercial Crew Program - The Next Big Thing

Siddhant Singh on March 23, 2019 at 4:37 pm

Launched by NASA in 2010, the commercial crew program is the next big step in the aerospace industry. It intends to replace the recently shut down Space Shuttle Program, launched in 1972 by NASA to utilize vehicles for transporting crew and cargo â€“ known as crew vehicles - to the low Earth orbit.

The program, funded by the U.S Government and administered by NASA, aims to expand and develop the commercial space industry to achieve safe, cost effective access to low Earth orbit. In 2014, in the final phase of the program, contracts were given to SpaceX and Boeing to each supply six flights to the International Space Station (ISS) between 2019 and 2024.

The crew vehicles have already been imagined and implemented by the two companies. Dragon Version 2 is the name of the crew vehicle manufactured by SpaceX. It holds the capacity to carry up to 7 crew members and a maximum takeoff weight of up to 6,500 kg. CST-100 Starliner is the name of the crew vehicle manufactured by Boeing, which also has the capacity to carry up to 7 crew members and maximum takeoff weight of up to 13,000 kg.
