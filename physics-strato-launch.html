Stratolaunch Flies the World’s New Largest Plane

by Salil Sodhi

On April 13th, an extraordinarily magnificent plane took to the skies from Mojave Spaceport in the Mojave Desert, California. The area, considered a grail for aerospace pioneering, was also home to Scaled Composites’s Spaceship One (the one that won the Ansari XPRIZE* in 2004). Stratolaunch’s plane, nicknamed ROC (ROcket Carrier), was also built by Scaled Composites and has set the new world-record for the ‘Largest Plane Built’ till date.



The new plane has an incredible 117-meter wingspan, more than 20m longer than the previous record holder. The plane reached speeds upwards of 300 kmph and an altitude of 17,000 feet on its maiden flight. ROC is designed to carry an astonishing 250-ton payload to 35,000 feet, where specially designed rockets, fitted in the centre of the wing, will be launched to low Earth orbit.

Pilot Evan Thomas said “The flight itself was smooth. The airplane flew as predicted, which is exactly what you want on a test-flight.” He described the systems working harmoniously together, similar to the running of a watch.

As testing continues, the ROC will be pushed to his limit; however, Scaled Composites has already pushed the limits of aerospace engineering by building and flying a plane of epic proportions, the ROC. The first flight of ROC is a message to the world that the race to commercialize space has begun.
