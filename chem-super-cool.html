One More Way To Blow Stuff Up!

by Salil Sodhi on Feb 23, 2019 at 1:39 pm

Sorry folks! Nothing’s catching on fire or blowing up this time. But I think things are about to get pretty cool. Check this out! https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5T68TvdoSbI



If you don’t find that cool, then I don’t know what is. How does a liquid seemingly freeze the moment it touches something? Can any liquid do that? Does it work with any liquid? Can I change the phase of anything I touch? (yes) Hold your horses!

What we see here is the phenomenon of super-cooled water. The water is actually below its freezing point but it hasn’t changed its phase yet. In this case, the water freezes upon contact with the bowl; layers of ice build up on this first layer of ice. And lo and behold, you have your very own ice mountain.



Materials required:

  1. 1) Distilled water or a soft drink (Sprite, Coke, etc.).
  2. 2) A freezer.
  3. 3) A bowl to pour the supercool[ed] liquid into.
  4. 4) Some thick gloves to prevent frostbite.
  5. 5) A mind ready to be blown.

The procedure: Take the liquid and cool it to just below the freezing point of water (anything below 0 degrees Celsius but make sure the liquid hasn’t frozen yet). Carefully take the liquid out of the freezer and pour it slowly into the bowl. Observe as the mountain of ice grows bigger. (Try to capture your expression when you see it for the first time. Trust me. It’ll be hilarious.) Do it as many times as you want. It never gets old.
