Something New About Black holes

Kashwi Aggarwal on Feb 18, 2019 at 10:08 am

Spaghettification - yes, scientists could not think of a better term. Yes, it is a real scientific term. Spaghettification is what happens to objects when they enter a black hole. Like the name suggests, the objects turn into spaghetti. Well, as much as you love spaghetti, this is not the kind you can eat. In fact, it is not real spaghetti at all. Objects are simply stretched to the extent that they can be equated to noodles.



The way all of this works is to do with the behaviour of gravity, a skillful chef. At this moment, as you read, your feet are closer to your head than they are to the center of the Earth. The difference in the attraction between your ‘head and feet’ and your ‘feet and earth’s core’ helps you not get stretched and die. This difference in attraction reverses when you enter a black hole. It works against your favour. Your feet are more attracted to the ‘singularity’ - center of the black hole. The massive gravitational force exerted by the black hole pulls your feet away from your head, turning you into spaghetti.

The original idea was to encase the whole star in a megastructure to capture its power output. A solid shell enveloping the sun is probably not the way to go, as such a large body is vulnerable to collisions with shattering outer space debris. Not to mention, the building resources required to build an entire dome structure around the sun is incomprehensible and requires more material than is available in the solar system.

So, you might just want to stay away from black holes while travelling in your spaceship.
